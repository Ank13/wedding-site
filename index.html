Planes

The most convienent airport is Chicago O'Hare International Airport (ORD), which hosts most major airlines.

If you are a Southwest Airlines frequent flier, then you might consider Chicago Midway International Airport (MDW), but it is much farther from Highland Park than O'Hare.

American Taxi is a special service to the suburbs of Chicago. Make a reservation or call from the airport when you arrive.

Trains

Chicago's Metra runs between downtown Chicago and Highland Park. Get on at Ogilvie Transportation Center (buy tickets in station to avoid surcharge), and get off at Ravinia (not Ravinia Park). The ride is approximately 45 minutes on a Sunday morning, and one-way ticket costs $5.25. Ravinia station is 0.7 miles from the synagogue. Either walk, or contact us to arrange a ride.

Automobiles

Rather than paying for cabs to and from the airport, you might consider renting a car during your stay, especially if you’ll be exploring the Chicago area over the weekend.

Guide for the Perplexed

Understanding Jewish Wedding Traditions

Kabbalat Panim

Shira will be sitting on a chair, symbolizing a throne, as she begins her role as the “queen” for the day. She will be surrounded by the mothers, grandmothers, sisters, and bridemaids. It is customary for guests to greet Shira and rejoice with her.

Tisch

Avi, the “king” will command his own room where he will be surrounded by the fathers, brothers, and other friends. During this time some important legal and ritual documents will be signed.

Everyone is encouraged to walk between the two rooms to greet both Shira and Avi. Appetizers will be served during this time.

Bedeken

At the conclusion of Avi’s tisch, he will be escorted - to the sound of festive music - by the fathers, family, and friends to see Shira. People are encouraged to join in the singing and dancing. Avi performs the bedeken - he “checks” to confirm that the bride is Shira and then will cover her face with her veil. It is also customary for Shira to receive blessings from the fathers at this time.

Chuppah

The guests will then find their way to the synagogue’s sanctuary for the ceremony. Shira and Avi will be married under a chuppah, which symbolizes the home they will build together. It is open on all four sides to represent their open, welcoming, and hospitable home. The ceremony will be conducted by Shira’s father, Rabbi Vernon Kurtz.

Yichud

At the conclusion of the ceremony (after Avi breaks the glass), Shira and Avi will leave the chuppah surrounded by friends and family who are singing and dancing to festive music. They are escorted to a private room where they will share their first moments together as a married couple.

Simcha (Reception)

The guests are invited into the reception hall to begin their first course. After their private time together, Shira and Avi will join their guests in the hall for the celebration, beginning with traditional Jewish music and dancing (hora). It is a great mitzvah to dance before the bride and groom and entertain them. Shira and Avi will rejoice with their guests through dancing and partaking in the wedding feast.

Mezinka

During the reception, Bryna and Vernon (Shira’s parents) and Carol and Joel (Avi’s parents) will take seats on the dance floor and will be presented with flower crowns by their children. They will be honored in this traditional way for marrying off their last children. Family and friends will circle them with dance and people are encouraged to give each one a kiss and wish them a mazel tov.
